Dominion Energy

In the summer before my sophomore year in high school I had the tremendous opportunity to intern at Dominion Energy. Myself and 5 others were temporarily hired to mainly perform safety checks on every employee in all of their Richmond corporate offices. After we finished the safety checks in the first 2 weeks I helped the I.T. department with help desk tickets, mainly hardware related. As well as working on help desk tickets I helped them flash new laptops with a company wide version of Windows, and clean retired laptops using a kill disk.





Internship

Business Intelligence/Analytics

Being my first department in the MaxxPower rotational work I wasn’t sure what I’d be challenged with. My role being a Data Engineer I was the person who implemented Google Analytics into the Company website as well as manage the websites traffic. Along with being the Data Engineer I was tasked with helping the Data Analyst with any problems they ran into while making dashboards in Google Data Studio. During this rotation I became familiar with Google Analytics and Google Data Studio so that not only I could complete my task, but as well as help the others in my group.





System/App Development

My second rotation I was assigned to the Sys/App Development department, my individual role was Scrum Master/Product Owner. Tasked with a full website overhaul of the company website I decided to plan the 2 week sprint on Trello so that the entire team could have access to each days workload. Sadly enough we did not get to the point that I had intended us to be at for the finish of the project, but we did rebuild the website to make it more professional. I did not plan for any days off when planning the 2 week sprint, and in the second week we had multiple days off that I didn’t account for. That being said the project was still complete and on time. Even with the setbacks this rotation was easily my favorite, being able to experience an Agile inspired 2 week sprint and working as a machine and not as individual people felt amazing to lead/be a part of.





Information Security

My third and final department being Information Security I knew that It was going to be a challenge because the other 2 groups that went before were unable to complete the project at hand. In the position of System Administrator and tasked with implementing a password management system my biggest role was to decide what each department was able to see in the password management system. Choosing to implement a fully built password management system rather than build one I feel was the correct decision considering the technical skill in my group and the goals of the project. Another challenge was to find a password management system for free, in the end we decided on Dashlane because it was easy to use, free of cost, and fit the needs of the company.
